📖 New to trading? Read this first (2 minutes)

The only 3 rules that matter

1. Never risk more than 1% of your money on one trade. Use the position-size calculator in the Quant Lab — it does the math for you.
2. Decide your exit before you enter. Every trade plan here comes with a stop loss. If price hits it, you sell. No "maybe it comes back."
3. Never add money to a losing trade. Averaging down feels smart and is the #1 account killer.

Swing vs. Invest — which are you doing?

Swing = renting a stock for 2–6 weeks to catch a move. You live by the stop loss. Invest = marrying it for years because you believe in the business. You buy slowly (DCA) and ignore the noise. Decide before you buy — most losses come from swings that turn into accidental "investments."

How to read the colors

🟢 Buy zone — a real setup exists, with entry/stop/targets. · 🟡 Wait — promising but not confirmed. · ⚪ Range — sideways, skip it. · 🔴 Avoid — still falling; cheap can get cheaper.

Quick glossary

PnL — profit and loss. "Unrealized" = on paper; "realized" = you actually sold.
Stop loss — the price where you admit you're wrong and sell. Set it BEFORE buying.
RSI — momentum meter 0–100. Above 70 = maybe too hot; below 30 = beaten up.
EMA — moving average, the trend line. Price above rising EMAs = healthy.
ATR — how much it typically moves per day. Sets how wide your stop must be.
ADX — trend strength. Above 25 = real trend; below 20 = choppy mess.
R — your risk unit (entry − stop). Target 1 = you make 1.5× what you risked.
Drawdown — worst drop from a peak. Crypto regularly does −50%. Size accordingly.
Lot — IDX stocks trade in lots of 100 shares.
DCA — buying a fixed amount monthly regardless of price. The investor's tool.
